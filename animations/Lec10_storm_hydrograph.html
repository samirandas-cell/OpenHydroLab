Storm Hydrograph

CVE2142 · Lecture 10 · Rain falls on a catchment and the stream responds — the hydrograph is computed live from a losses + linear-reservoir routing model

Storm

Rain below φ soaks in (→ groundwater); rain above φ is effective rainfall (→ direct runoff).

Catchment

Storage constant of each routing reservoir — big/flat/vegetated basins store more (large k); small/steep basins respond fast.
Paving reduces infiltration (φ↓) and speeds the response (k↓) — Lecture 10's "influence of urbanization".

Show

Aha demo: press Play and watch the stream in the scene rise with the tracing curve — like the Carbondale time-lapse from the lecture. Then slide Urbanization to 80% with the baseline overlay on: same storm, but the peak is higher, earlier and flashier, and the slow baseflow hump shrinks — the paved catchment converts groundwater recharge into flood peak.
