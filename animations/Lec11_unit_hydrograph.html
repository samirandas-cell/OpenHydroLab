Storm pattern

Changing Dᵣ automatically switches to the Dᵣ-hour UH (built by the S-curve method of mode 3) — a UH may only be applied to pulses of its own duration.

Show

Aha demo: the total flood is the colored bands stacked — each band is one rain pulse pushed through the same UH, lagged by when it fell. Drag R₂ to zero and back: its whole band vanishes while the others stand still (linearity + superposition). Then shorten Dᵣ: the same rain depths arrive faster, the UH in use gets peakier, and the flood peak climbs.

Target duration

Show

Aha demo: drag D′ from 1 h to 24 h and watch the UH morph: short-duration UHs are tall and peaky, long ones low and flat, volume pinned at 1 cm. At D′ = 12, 18, 24 h the superposition method (sum n lagged 6-h UHs, ÷ n) draws its own white dashed curve — it lands exactly on the S-curve result, because superposition IS the S-curve algebra restricted to integer multiples. Now set D′ = 9 h: superposition has no answer, and that is precisely why the S-curve method exists.
