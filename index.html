OpenHydroLab

Physics-accurate, interactive animations for teaching hydraulics & hydrology

OpenHydroLab is a free library of single-file, interactive HTML animations for undergraduate hydraulics and hydrology. Every number on screen is computed live from the real governing equations — no faked motion. Each animation runs in any modern browser, needs no installation, and can be projected in class or explored by students at home.

Open-channel hydraulics

Channel Geometry & Froude Number 3D

Channel cross-section shapes with live A, P, R, T, D; selectable velocity profiles with α and β integrated numerically (self-checking); plan-view stone-ripple demo where ripples spread at c = √(gD) while advected at V — subcritical flow shows the upstream "news front" at c − V, supercritical folds into the Froude wedge sin θ = 1/Fr, the open-channel Mach cone.

Launch

Needs internet on first load (3D library via CDN); all other animations are fully offline.

Hydrology

Rainfall Frequency & IDF Curves 2D · offline

Raw gauge to design discharge on real data (Drumalbin, 27 yr hourly): slide the annual-maximum window, fit Gumbel by moments on probability paper, build the IDF family with a Sherman equation, then bridge to the rational method — Qₚ(D) peaks exactly at t꜀.

Launch

Storm Hydrograph 2D · offline

Watch a storm rain on a catchment and the stream rise, peak and recede — rising limb, lag time, baseflow separation and mass balance all computed live from a losses + linear-reservoir routing model, with an urbanization comparison.

Launch

Unit Hydrograph Workbench 2D · offline

The catchment as a linear system, in three modes: derive a UH from an observed storm (baseflow separation with a reconciliation check), predict a flood by superposition (stacked bands), and change duration with the S-curve — drag D′ and watch the UH morph while its volume stays exactly 1 cm.

Launch

In final verification, released as they are confirmed: Manning uniform flow, specific energy & critical depth, hydraulic jump, gradually varied flow profiles, water balance, the rational method, and steady flow to a well. Also in development: weirs and sluice gates, channel transitions, Thiessen polygons, and unsteady well drawdown (Theis).

Physics integrity

Each animation is built to a strict rule: every displayed quantity is computed from the governing equation (continuity, energy, momentum, Manning, the unit-hydrograph and frequency-analysis relations), and results are verified by hand against worked examples before release. These are teaching instruments, not illustrations.
